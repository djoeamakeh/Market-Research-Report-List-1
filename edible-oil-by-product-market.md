<p><p>The edible oil by-product market consists of two primary types: liquid co-products and solid co-products. Liquid co-products, such as crude glycerin and fatty acids, are derived from the refining and extraction processes of oils and are often used in food, cosmetics, and biofuel industries. Solid co-products, including oil cakes and meal, result from the extraction of oil from seeds and are typically utilized as animal feed or in fertilizer production. Both types provide value-added opportunities in various sectors.</p></p>
