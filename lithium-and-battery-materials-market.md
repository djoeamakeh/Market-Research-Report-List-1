<p><p>The lithium and battery materials market encompasses several key components essential for battery functionality. Anode materials, commonly graphite or silicon, store lithium ions during charging. Negative electrode materials, often in the form of lithium metal or alloy, enhance energy density. Electrolytes, typically lithium salts dissolved in solvents, facilitate ionic movement between electrodes. Diaphragms, or separators, prevent short circuits by separating the anode and cathode while allowing ionic flow. Together, these components drive the efficiency and performance of lithium-ion batteries.</p></p>
