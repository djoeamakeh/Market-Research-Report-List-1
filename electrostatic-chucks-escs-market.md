<p><p>Electrostatic Chucks (ESCs) are devices used in semiconductor manufacturing to hold wafers in place during processing. The two main market types are the Coulomb Force Type and the Johnson-Rahbeck Force Type. The Coulomb Force Type utilizes static electric charges to secure wafers, offering strong adhesion with minimal mechanical stress. In contrast, the Johnson-Rahbeck Force Type employs a combination of electric and mechanical forces for improved stability and alignment, catering to specific application needs within the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
